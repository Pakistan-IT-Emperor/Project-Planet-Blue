Information

In 1900, the concentration of carbon dioxide (CO2) in the atmosphere was 291 parts per million (ppm). This was an increase from 288 ppm in 1860.

The amount of CO2 in the atmosphere has increased since the start of the Industrial Revolution in 1750. This increase is due to the burning of fossil fuels for energy and the growth of the population. In 1900, the world released almost 2 billion metric tons of CO2 from fossil fuels. By 1960, that number had more than quadrupled.

Monthly mean atmospheric carbon dioxide (CO₂) concentrations reached a record high of 426.91 parts per million (ppm) in June 2024. This represents an increase of roughly 20 percent compared with average CO₂ levels recorded in June 1990. In 2023, global average annual CO₂ concentrations reached a record high of 421 ppm.

Global Warming: Understanding the Crisis What is Global Warming? Global warming refers to the gradual increase in the Earth’s average surface temperature due to human activities that increase levels of greenhouse gases in the atmosphere. Since the Industrial Revolution, human actions—such as burning fossil fuels, deforestation, and intensive agriculture—have led to significant changes in the environment. These changes trap heat in the Earth's atmosphere, leading to rising temperatures worldwide.


Causes of Global Warming

1. Burning Fossil Fuels: Coal, oil, and natural gas are burned to produce energy, releasing carbon dioxide (CO₂) and other greenhouse gases.
2. Deforestation: Trees absorb CO₂, so cutting them down means more CO₂ remains in the atmosphere, enhancing the greenhouse effect.
3. Agriculture and Livestock: Intensive farming and livestock practices release large amounts of methane, a potent greenhouse gas.
4. Industrial Processes: Many manufacturing processes emit various greenhouse gases that contribute to warming the planet.


Effects of Global Warming

Global warming has far-reaching effects that impact every aspect of life on Earth.
Some key effects include:

  • Melting Ice and Rising Sea Levels: The warming climate melts polar ice caps and glaciers, causing sea levels to rise, which threatens coastal communities and habitats.

  • Extreme Weather Events: Global warming contributes to more frequent and severe weather events, such as hurricanes, droughts, wildfires, and heavy rains.

  • Ecosystem Disruption: Rising temperatures and changing weather patterns disrupt ecosystems, pushing many species toward extinction and affecting biodiversity.

  • Health Risks: Warmer temperatures can worsen air quality, leading to respiratory issues. There is also an increased risk of heat-related illnesses and the spread of infectious diseases.

What Can We Do to Stop Global Warming?


Though global warming is a serious issue, there are steps we can take to reduce its effects:

1. Shift to Renewable Energy: Replacing fossil fuels with renewable energy sources like wind, solar, and hydroelectric power can reduce greenhouse gas emissions.

2. Energy Efficiency: Using energy-efficient appliances and reducing energy consumption helps lower emissions.

3. Protect and Restore Forests: Forests act as carbon sinks, absorbing CO₂ from the atmosphere. Protecting existing forests and planting new trees are crucial.

4. Sustainable Agriculture: Reducing emissions from agricultural practices, supporting sustainable farming, and shifting towards plant-based diets can make a difference.

5. Support Policy Changes: Advocating for environmental policies and supporting international agreements like the Paris Agreement can help address global warming on a larger scale.
